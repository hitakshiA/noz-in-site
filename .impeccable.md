## Design Context

### Users
Observability / SRE engineers, SigNoz maintainers, and hackathon judges evaluating
noz-in — a tool that migrates Grafana dashboards into SigNoz. They read fast, are
technical, and are skeptical of hype. The page is the "deployed link" for a
hackathon submission and the first impression of the project.

### Brand Personality
Honest, precise, technical, credible — "a SigNoz engineer built this." Confidence
without marketing gloss. The product's whole thesis is *proof over claims*, so the
page must feel measured, not loud. Three words: honest, exact, native.

### Aesthetic Direction
SigNoz-native dark UI. Real SigNoz brand tokens: Ink #0b0c0e surface, Robin blue
#4e74f8 primary, Aqua #23c4f8 accent. Signature moment: the Grafana→noz-in→SigNoz
icon pipeline with an animated beam (migration flowing left→right, turning
SigNoz-blue). Dark theme is correct for this audience/context (observability, SRE).
Anti-references: generic SaaS marketing pages, AI-slop (gradient text, neon glow
for its own sake), anything that reads as a "toy."

### Design Principles
1. Space communicates hierarchy — generous between sections, tight within groups.
2. Every visual claim is real (real logos, real brand tokens); nothing decorative-fake.
3. Restraint over intensity — the arc + beam are the one flourish; everything else is calm.
4. Solid color for text; weight and space carry emphasis, never gradient fill.
5. One idea per moment — a single centered hero, not a wall of cards.
